COLLEGE DIRECTORY

College

College

A college is an institution of higher education that typically offers undergraduate degrees (such as associate's or bachelor's degrees) in a variety of academic disciplines. Some colleges may also offer limited graduate programs, but they are generally smaller and more focused than universities.
University

University

A university is a larger institution of higher education that offers both undergraduate and graduate degrees, including master’s and doctoral programs. Universities are often made up of multiple colleges or schools, each focusing on a specific field of study, and they often prioritize both teaching and research.
School of Music

School of Music

A school of music is a specialized division within a college or university dedicated to the study of music. It offers programs in areas such as performance, composition, music education, and music theory, and often leads to degrees at the undergraduate and graduate levels. These schools may be part of a larger liberal arts or research institution.
Conservatory

Conservatory

A conservatory is an institution focused almost exclusively on the study and performance of the arts—primarily music, but often including dance, drama, and related fields. Conservatories provide intensive, performance-based training and are typically more selective and focused than general schools of music.

Pioneers in American Music Education: Key Milestones and Institutions

1636

Harvard University

Established in 1636, required music reading in its first curriculum and created America's first student orchestra in 1808.

1833

Boston Academy of Music

Founded by Lowell Mason and George James Webb, it was the first school of higher musical education.

1845

University of Notre Dame

Formed the first performing band in 1845, establishing the foundation for collegiate marching bands.

1858

Harvard Glee Club

Founded in 1858, it is the oldest college chorus in the United States.

1865

Oberlin Conservatory

Oberlin College established the Conservatory of Music in 1865, pioneering conservatory education.

1876

Boston University

Awarded the first recorded bachelor's degree in music in 1876, setting the standard for music degrees.

1884

SUNY Potsdam

The Potsdam Normal School (now SUNY Potsdam) started the first training program for public school music teachers in 1884.
